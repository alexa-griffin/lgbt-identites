Orientations

An Orientation (sometimes referred to as a sexual orientation, though it does not have to be sexual in nature) is what you like. This generally falls into 3 categories: romantic, sexual, and the ace/aro spectrum. Sexual and romantic attraction are self explanatory with sexual being that which one is aroused by and romantic being that which one loves, generally speaking for any *-sexual there is also a *-romantic and vice versa. The ace/aro (asexual/arosexual) specturm is the orientation lacking one or both of those.

Homosexual

Sexually attracted only to the same gender as yourself.

Homoromantic

Romantically attracted only to the same gender as yourself.

Hetrosexual

Sexually attracted only to a different gender as yourself.

Hetroromantic

Romantically attracted only to a different gender as yourself.

Bisexual

Sexually attracted to two genders.

Biromantic

Romantically attracted to two genders.

*There is some controversy here as some choose to use it for 2 or more genders.

Polysexual

Sexually attracted to many but not all genders.

Polyromantic

Romantically attracted to many but not all genders.

Pansexual

Sexually attracted to all genders.

Panromantic

Romantically attracted to all genders.

Skoliosexual

Sexually attracted to non-binary genders.

*There is controversy with this term being appropriate or not.

Acesexual

Not sexually attracted to any gender.

Aroromantic

Not romantically attracted to any gender.

Gynesexual

Sexually attracted to women/feminine-identified people.

Gyneromantic

Romantically attracted to women/feminine-identified people.

Androsexual

Sexually attracted to men/masculine-identified people.

Androromantic

Romantically attracted to men/masculine-identified people.
